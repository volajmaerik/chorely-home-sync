Chorely: A Gamified Approach to Household Management

A Seminar Paper Presentation

Authors: Adam Hlavatý, Erik Kováč, Martin Prošek, Erik Tomko

Course: Gamification

Date: October 13, 2025

Project Goal

  • The Problem: Managing household chores in shared living spaces is a common source of conflict, unfair workloads, and strained relationships.
  • The Objective: To apply gamification principles to transform mundane household tasks into an engaging, competitive, and rewarding experience.
  • The Vision: To foster a cleaner, more collaborative, and positive living environment by motivating users through a structured and interactive system.

Target Audience

  • Primary Demographic: Young adults aged 18-28, including university students and young professionals.
  • Living Situation: Individuals in shared housing, dormitories, or co-living communities.
  • User Profile: Tech-savvy individuals who are motivated by social recognition, achievement, and friendly competition. They value experiences and collaborative environments.

Key Features

Household Management

Users can create or join households with unique invite codes, with a designated admin to manage members and settings.

Dynamic Chore System

A shared list of chores that can be claimed, completed, and suggested by any member, keeping the system relevant and interactive.

Gamified Loop

A core system of points, seasonal leaderboards, and peer evaluations transforms chore completion into a competitive and rewarding game.

Gamification Strategy

Points & Progress (Competence)

Chores have point values that provide immediate, tangible feedback. This fosters a sense of accomplishment and mastery over the "game."

Leaderboards & Seasons (Relatedness)

A seasonal leaderboard creates friendly competition and social context. Periodic resets keep the game fair and engaging for everyone.

Peer Evaluation (Social Proof)

A 1-5 star rating system encourages high-quality work and modifies point rewards. It creates a self-sustaining feedback loop.

User Choice (Autonomy)

Users choose which chores to claim and can suggest new ones, providing a sense of control and ownership over the system.

Future Improvements

Achievement System

Introduce badges and awards for reaching milestones (e.g., "Clean Streak") to provide long-term goals and appeal to Achievers.

Cooperative Goals

Implement team-based challenges for the entire household to encourage collaboration over pure competition.

Financial Integration

Expand the app's scope to include tools for managing shared expenses, such as groceries or utility bills.

Conclusion

  • A Successful Proof-of-Concept: Chorely effectively demonstrates how gamification can solve a common, real-world problem.
  • Psychologically Grounded: The app's design is rooted in Self-Determination Theory, satisfying user needs for **autonomy, competence, and relatedness**.
  • Positive Behavioral Design: The core gameplay loop fosters accountability, encourages quality work, and promotes a sense of shared responsibility.
  • An Exemplar: Chorely serves as a strong example of how technology can be engineered to create more positive human interactions.
