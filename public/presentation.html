The Problem with Chores in Shared Living

  • The Core Conflict: The management of household chores is a primary source of friction in co-living environments like university dorms and shared apartments.
  • Why Traditional Methods Fail:
    • Diffusion of Responsibility: When a task belongs to everyone, it belongs to no one.
    • Lack of Accountability: Informal agreements are easily forgotten or ignored.
    • No Positive Feedback: Effort often goes unrecognized, leading to diminished motivation.
  • The Result: Unfair workload distribution, strained housemate relationships, and a negative living environment.

A Modern Solution: Gamification

  • What is Gamification? The use of game design elements and mechanics in non-game contexts to motivate and engage people (Deterding et al., 2011).
  • The Goal: To transform mundane, real-world tasks into more engaging and rewarding experiences.
  • Key Game Elements:
    • Points & Progress Bars
    • Leaderboards & Rankings
    • Achievements & Badges
    • Immediate Feedback Loops

The Psychology of Motivation

Self-Determination Theory (Ryan & Deci, 2000)

Autonomy

The need to feel in control of one's own actions and choices. The feeling that you are the author of your own life.

Competence

The need to feel effective and capable of mastering tasks. It's the satisfaction of progress and achievement.

Relatedness

The need to feel connected to others and part of a community. It's about belonging and mutual respect.

Our Thesis: A successful gamified system must be designed to satisfy these three fundamental needs.

Introducing Chorely: The Application

  • What is Chorely? A responsive web application designed to gamify household chore management.
  • Target Audience: University students and young professionals in shared living situations.
  • Design Philosophy: To provide a clear, structured, and positive platform that reduces the stress associated with chores. The interface is modern, intuitive, and mobile-first.

Gamification in Detail (1/4)

Fostering Competence

  • Immediate Feedback: Points are awarded upon completion, turning a thankless task into a measurable achievement.
  • Sense of Mastery: Users can track their cumulative score, providing a tangible sense of progress and validating their efforts.
  • Appeals to Achievers: This system directly motivates users who are driven by tangible markers of success (Bartle, 1996).

Gamification in Detail (2/4)

Driving Competition and Relatedness

  • Social Context: The leaderboard makes individual contributions visible to everyone, creating friendly competition.
  • Fairness with "Seasons": The leaderboard resets periodically, giving everyone a fresh start and preventing discouragement.
  • Extrinsic Motivation: An optional, admin-defined prize pool adds a tangible goal (Marczewski, 2015).

Gamification in Detail (3/4)

A Feedback and Social Proof Loop

  • Quality Control: Knowing work will be rated encourages users to maintain a high standard.
  • Dynamic Rewards: Final points are modified based on the average star rating. High-quality work earns bonus points.
  • Incentivized Participation: Evaluators earn a small number of points for rating others, strengthening the sense of community.

Gamification in Detail (4/4)

Cultivating Autonomy

  • Freedom of Choice: Unlike rigid rotas, Chorely allows users to claim chores that fit their own schedules.
  • Increased Buy-In: This sense of control reframes chores from an obligation to a volitional act.
  • Co-Creation: The "Suggest a Chore" feature empowers users to actively shape the rules of the game.

Technical Architecture

Frontend

  • React & TypeScript: For a modern, type-safe, and responsive user interface.
  • shadcn/ui & Tailwind CSS: For a sleek, customizable, and professional design system.

Backend

  • Supabase: An open-source platform providing:
  • – PostgreSQL Database
  • – User Authentication
  • – Auto-generated RESTful APIs

Key Feature: Real-time database capabilities ensure that all feedback, such as point updates and leaderboard changes, is instantaneous.

Discussion and Future Scope

Potential Impact

Aims to reduce household conflict and increase collaboration by making chores transparent and rewarding.

Limitations & Ethics

The system must be balanced to avoid negative competition. It is a tool to augment, not replace, direct communication.

Future Enhancements

  • Achievement System (Badges)
  • Cooperative Team Goals
  • Shared Financial Tracking

Conclusion

  • A Successful Proof-of-Concept: Chorely effectively demonstrates how gamification principles can be applied to solve a common, real-world problem.
  • Psychologically Grounded: The application's design is rooted in Self-Determination Theory, by satisfying users' needs for **autonomy, competence, and relatedness**.
  • Positive Behavioral Design: The core gameplay loop creates a powerful motivational structure that fosters accountability, encourages quality work, and promotes a sense of shared responsibility.
  • An Exemplar: Chorely serves as a strong example of how technology can be thoughtfully engineered to architect more positive and harmonious human interactions.
